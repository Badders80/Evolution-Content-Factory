## Script Structure

### **Hook** (0-5 seconds)
"They said [HE/SHE] was [TOO SMALL / TOO SLOW / FINISHED]. The data said otherwise."

*[Alternative hooks]*:
- "Written off at $[PRICE]. But the numbers told a different story."
- "The experts doubted. The sectionals didn't."
- "[YEAR]. [HORSE NAME]. Everyone said no. The data said yes."

---

### **The Story** (5-30 seconds)

**The Setup**:
- [Horse] was [underdog situation - e.g., "bought for $5,000 at yearling sales"]
- [Context - e.g., "small frame, unfashionable pedigree"]
- [What experts said - actual quotes if available]

**The Data That Mattered**:
- [What the numbers showed - stride efficiency, sectional times]
- [What others missed - hidden indicator]
- [Why the data said there was more]

**The Moment**:
- [The race where it happened - Group race, upset win]
- [Result - paid $[PRICE], beat $[FAVORITE PRICE] favorite]
- [Outcome - where the horse ended up]

---

### **Close** (30-40 seconds)
"Sometimes the best stories are written by those no one expected."

*[Alternative closes]*:
- "The data doesn't care about pedigree. It cares about performance."
- "[HORSE NAME]. Proof that numbers don't lie."
- "From $[LOW PRICE] to Group [X] winner. That's racing."

## Voice Guidelines

### OK Do
- Tell emotional story (this is the one place for feeling)
- Ground emotion in DATA (stride efficiency, sectionals)
- Inspire ("anyone can beat the odds with right data")

### NO Don't
- Make it pure emotion without data backup
- Over-sentimentalize ("heart of a champion")
- Lose the data-driven edge

## Story Sources

**Where to find underdog stories**:
- NZ racing history archives
- TAB upset results (high-priced winners)
- Trainer/owner interviews (with permission)
- Racing media archives (past years)

**Criteria for selection**:
- Clear underdog narrative (written off, doubted)
- Data that should have indicated otherwise
- Inspiring outcome (Group win, upset victory)
- NZ-specific (local connection)
